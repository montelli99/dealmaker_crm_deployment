Dealmaker Business Model Overview

Core Knowledge Last updated: Apr 9, 2025

The Dealmaker business model is built on a dual revenue stream approach that aligns incentives with client success while providing consistent cash flow. This model has been proven to generate significant revenue while delivering exceptional value to clients.

Key Components
Engagement Fees

Upfront fees ranging from $10,000-$50,000 that cover initial work and create commitment. These fees provide immediate cash flow and qualify serious clients.

Success Fees

Performance-based fees typically 2-5% of transaction value, creating alignment with client outcomes and significant upside potential.

Service Tiers
Tier Engagement Fee Success Fee Target Client
Standard $10,000 - $25,000 5% of transaction value Small businesses ($1M-$5M)
Premium $25,000 - $50,000 3-4% of transaction value Mid-market companies ($5M-$50M)
Enterprise $50,000+ 2-3% of transaction value Large enterprises ($50M+)
Implementation Strategy
  1. Value-Based Positioning: Focus on ROI and outcomes rather than hourly rates
  2. Clear Deliverables: Define specific deliverables for engagement fees
  3. Milestone Payments: Structure engagement fees in 2-3 milestone payments
  4. Success Definition: Clearly define what constitutes "success" for performance fees
  5. Advisor Network: Leverage advisors to enhance credibility and service delivery

Advisor Network Development

Strategy Last updated: Apr 7, 2025

Building a network of specialized advisors is a cornerstone of the Dealmaker methodology. This approach enhances credibility, expands service capabilities, and creates additional revenue streams through fee-sharing arrangements.

Advisor Categories
Industry Experts

Specialists with deep knowledge in specific industries who can provide insights and credibility.

Technical Specialists

Experts in specific technical areas such as valuation, tax, legal, or due diligence.

Capital Sources

Investors, lenders, and other capital providers who can facilitate transactions.

Fee-Sharing Structure

The recommended fee-sharing structure allocates 20-30% of success fees to advisors who contribute significantly to deal completion. This creates alignment while maintaining profitability.
